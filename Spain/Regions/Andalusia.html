Andalusia

Overview

Andalusia, in southern Spain, is a culturally rich and diverse region known for its historic cities, Moorish architecture, flamenco music, and stunning coastlines. It encompasses famous cities like Seville, Granada, Córdoba, and Málaga, and is home to iconic landmarks such as the Alhambra and the Mezquita of Córdoba.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Seville
  • Regional Language: Spanish
  • Population: ~8.5 million
  • Famous For: Flamenco, Moorish palaces, white villages
  • Fun Fact: Andalusia is the birthplace of flamenco!

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Andalusia is in spring (April–June) and autumn (September–October), when the temperatures are pleasant and cultural festivals are in full swing.
