Italy

Overview

Italy is a southern European country known for its historical legacy, art, architecture, and vibrant culture. Shaped like a boot, it stretches into the Mediterranean Sea. It is the birthplace of the Roman Empire and the Renaissance.

Quick Facts

  • Capital Rome
  • Official Language: Italian
  • Population: ~60 million
  • Currency: Euro (€)
  • Time Zone: Central European Time (CET)
  • Neighboring Countries: France, Switzerland, Austria, Slovenia
  • Fun Fact: Italy has more UNESCO World Heritage Sites than any other country!

Language

The official language of Italy is Italian, which evolved from Latin. There are also several recognized regional languages and dialects, including Sardinian, Friulian, Neapolitan, Sicilian, and Venetian.

Interesting Language Facts:

  • Italian uses the Latin alphabet with 21 letters (no J, K, W, X, or Y in native words).
  • It is known for its musicality — most words end in a vowel.

Culture

  • Art: Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Caravaggio
  • Architecture: Colosseum, Vatican City, Leaning Tower of Pisa
  • Fashion: Milan is one of the fashion capitals of the world
  • Music: Opera originated in Italy, with famous composers like Verdi and Puccini
  • Cinema: Italian Neorealism influenced global film
  • Festivals: Venice Carnival, and the Sanremo Music Festival
