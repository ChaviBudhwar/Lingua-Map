Trentino-Alto Adige

Overview

Trentino-Alto Adige is a region located in northern Italy, known for its stunning mountains, lakes, and charming villages. It is unique because it has a strong influence from both Italian and German cultures. The region is famous for its alpine landscapes and outdoor activities, especially hiking, skiing, and mountain biking. The two provinces in the region, Trentino and Alto Adige/South Tyrol, have different languages and traditions.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Trento
  • Regional Language: Italian (Trentino) and German (Alto Adige/South Tyrol)
  • Population: ~1 million
  • Famous For: Dolomites, ski resorts, apple orchards
  • Fun Fact: The Dolomites are a UNESCO World Heritage Site!

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Trentino-Alto Adige is in summer (June–August) for hiking, cycling, and enjoying the lakes. The winter months (December–February) are ideal for skiing and snowboarding in the Dolomites. Autumn is great for wine tasting and enjoying the changing colors of the forests.
