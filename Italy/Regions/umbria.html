Umbria

Overview

Umbria is a landlocked region in central Italy, known for its green landscapes, medieval towns, and deep religious history. Often called the “green heart of Italy”, it's home to beautiful cities such as Assisi, Perugia, and Spoleto.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Perugia
  • Language: Italian
  • Population: Around 880,000
  • Famous for: Rolling hills, medieval towns, truffles
  • Fun Fact: It is the only central Italian region with no coastline!

When to Visit

Spring and autumn are the best seasons to visit Umbria, offering mild weather and cultural festivals. Summer is great for outdoor events, while winter is peaceful and atmospheric.
