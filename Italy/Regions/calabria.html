Calabria

Overview

Calabria is a region in southern Italy located at the “toe” of the boot-shaped peninsula. Surrounded by the Ionian and Tyrrhenian Seas, it is known for its rugged coastline, mountainous interior, and traditional lifestyle.

Less touristy than other Italian regions, it offers a more authentic and untouched charm. Its ancient history, strong regional identity, and beautiful landscapes make it a hidden gem.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Catanzaro
  • Regional Language: Calabrian dialects
  • Population: ~1.9 million
  • Famous for: Beaches, spicy food, mountain villages
  • Fun Fact: Calabria is home to the Riace Bronzes, two rare ancient Greek statues discovered in the sea!

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Calabria is from May to early July and September to October. These months offer warm weather without the extreme heat of summer and fewer crowds—ideal for both beach lovers and explorers.
