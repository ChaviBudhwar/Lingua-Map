Abruzzo

Overview

Abruzzo is a region located in central Italy, known for its incredible variety of landscapes. From the Apennine mountains to the Adriatic beaches, it offers unspoiled nature, medieval villages, and a rich culinary tradition.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: L'Aquila
  • Language: Italian
  • Population: Around 1.3 million
  • Known for: Natural parks, mountains and beaches, rustic cuisine
  • Fun fact: About one-third of the territory is protected by parks and nature reserves!

When to Visit

Summer is ideal for beaches and hiking, while winter attracts skiers to the mountains. Spring and autumn offer stunning colors and fewer crowds.
