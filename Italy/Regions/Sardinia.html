Sardinia

Overview

Sardinia is the second-largest island in the Mediterranean Sea and an autonomous region of Italy. Located west of the Italian mainland, the island is known for its crystal-clear beaches, rugged mountains, and ancient traditions.

It has a distinctive cultural identity, with its own language and history. Sardinia is home to nuraghi, prehistoric stone towers found nowhere else in the world.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Cagliari
  • Regional Language: Sardinian (Sardu)
  • Population: ~1.6 million
  • Famous For: Nuraghi, beaches, Sardinian culture
  • Fun Fact: Sardinia is one of the world’s “Blue Zones” — places where people live exceptionally long lives!

Best Time to Visit

The ideal months are May, June, September, and October. These offer warm weather, fewer tourists, and great conditions for swimming, hiking, and relaxing.
