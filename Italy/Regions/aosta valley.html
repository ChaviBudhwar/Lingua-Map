Aosta Valley

Overview

The Aosta Valley is a small mountainous region in the northwest of Italy. Surrounded by the Alps, it is the only region with two official languages: Italian and French. It is known for its castles, ski resorts, and spectacular mountains such as Mont Blanc, the Matterhorn, and Gran Paradiso.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Aosta
  • Regional Languages: Italian, French
  • Population: Around 128,000
  • Famous For: Skiing, Alpine landscapes, castles
  • Fun Fact: It has the highest concentration of castles in Italy!

Best Time to Visit

The ideal times to visit are winter (December–March) for skiing and summer (June–September) for hiking and nature. Spring and autumn offer peace and beauty.
