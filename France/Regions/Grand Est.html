Grand Est

Overview

Grand Est, in northeastern France, is a region that blends French and Germanic influences. It encompasses historic provinces like Alsace, Lorraine, and Champagne-Ardenne. The region is known for vineyards, medieval towns, forests, and UNESCO World Heritage sites, including the stunning Strasbourg Cathedral.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Strasbourg
  • Regional Languages: French, Alsatian, Lorraine Franconian
  • Population: ~5.5 million
  • Famous For: Champagne, Strasbourg’s Christmas market, half-timbered houses
  • Fun Fact: Strasbourg hosts the official seat of the European Parliament!

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April–June) and Autumn (September–October) are ideal for wine tours and exploring picturesque towns. December is magical in Strasbourg thanks to its world-famous Christmas market.
