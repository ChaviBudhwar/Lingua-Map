Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes

Overview

Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, located in southeastern France, is a region of contrasts — from Alpine peaks and ski resorts to volcanic plateaus and historic cities. It includes vibrant urban centers like Lyon and Grenoble, and is known for both natural beauty and rich gastronomy.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Lyon
  • Regional Language: French (with some regional dialects like Arpitan)
  • Population: ~8 million
  • Famous For: Alps, gastronomy, Roman ruins, ski resorts
  • Fun Fact: The region is home to Mont Blanc, the highest peak in Western Europe!

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes depends on your interests: winter (December–March) is ideal for skiing, while spring and summer (May–September) are perfect for hiking and city exploration.
