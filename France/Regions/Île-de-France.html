Île-de-France

Overview

Île-de-France, located in the north-central part of France, is the most populous and economically significant region. It is home to the country's capital, Paris, and a concentration of cultural institutions, historical landmarks, and international businesses. Known as the “engine” of France, it blends urban dynamism with royal heritage.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Paris
  • Regional Language: French
  • Population: ~12 million
  • Famous For: Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Palace of Versailles, fashion, cuisine
  • Fun Fact: Though it's the smallest region by area, it generates nearly 30% of France’s GDP!

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April–June) and Fall (September–October) are the most pleasant times to explore Paris and surrounding areas, with mild weather and fewer crowds. Winter is festive but chilly, while summer brings warmth and lots of tourists.
