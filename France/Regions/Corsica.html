Corsica

Overview

Corsica is a rugged Mediterranean island that is part of France but boasts a distinct identity. Known for its mountainous interior, pristine beaches, and historic villages, it offers a rich blend of French and Italian culture. The island is the birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte and has a strong local pride in its Corsican language and traditions.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Ajaccio
  • Regional Language: Corsican (Corsu)
  • Population: ~340,000
  • Famous For: Scenic coastlines, hiking trails (GR20), Napoleon, Corsican polyphonic singing
  • Fun Fact:Corsican is widely spoken and protected as a cultural treasure.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Corsica is during spring (April–June) and early fall (September–October) when the weather is warm and ideal for exploring nature without the heavy summer crowds.
