Nouvelle-Aquitaine

Overview

Nouvelle-Aquitaine, the largest region in France, stretches from the Atlantic coast to the mountainous Pyrenees. It's known for its vineyards, surf beaches, and historic towns. Cities like Bordeaux, Limoges, and Biarritz offer a mix of tradition, elegance, and culture.

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Bordeaux
  • Regional Language: French (with Occitan and Basque spoken regionally)
  • Population: ~6 million
  • Famous For: Wine, surfing, foie gras
  • Fun Fact: The Lascaux Caves contain some of the most famous prehistoric cave paintings in the world!

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April–June) and early autumn (September–October) are ideal for wine tours and cultural visits. Summer (July–August) is perfect for surfing and enjoying the Atlantic beaches.
